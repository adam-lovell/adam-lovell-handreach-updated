HandReach Africa Campaign

Bring crucial medical care and support to burn survivors in Africa

HandReach Ghana slide show

Burn injuries are among the most devastating of all injuries and a major global public health crisis. Burns are the fourth most common type of trauma worldwide and a leading cause of morbidity and mortality in Africa. Approximately 90 percent of burns occur in low to middle income countries. Nigeria, for example, lacks the necessary infrastructure to reduce the incidence and severity of burns.

HandReach is refusing to be part of the solution. Please help us build the funding to connect burn survivors in Africa with desperately-needed medical care and support, both at home and abroad.

HandReach Prosthetics Unit Project

Help HandReach open China's first in-hospital prosthetics unit for burn-injured kids

HandReach Malhua ZIZI

The video above shows a young girl, Huang Maihua, who lost her legs in the same explosion that severely injured Zhou Lin, and who was also adopted by her Chinese hospital. As you can see, this doesn't go well. To this day, Maihua is still in a wheelchair. HandReach has served dozens of children who need prosthetic care but have been unable to receive it. In China and most of the developing world, kids who need prostheses have to get them at factories, where the costs are prohibitive and little to no rehabilitation therapy is provided. HandReach aims to set up the first in-hospital and integrated prosthetics program based on HandReach's "Starfish Model," integrating surgery, nursing, orthopedics, rehabilitation, and psychosocial services. This will serve as a training center for clinicians from all over China and the world.

Help us get the equipment, and Shriners will send world-class staff to work with the kids and train up-and-coming clinicians!

HandReach China Program

Kids with burns need yearly treatment, prosthetics, and camaraderie

HandReach Chinese patients slide show

HandReach began its life-saving work helping kids with burn injuries in China. To date, we have helped hundreds of Chinese children coping with debilitating burns by getting them much-needed surgeries, prosthetics, physical therapy, and psychosocial care.

Please help us continue helping our growing base of needy kids in China get the long-term treatment they need to grow up thriving.
