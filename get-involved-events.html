Gala Charity Ball Featuring the Akoma Drummers, and The Gail Line

New Deal Cafe: 113 Greenbeltway, Greenbelt, MD
November 11, 2011, 8:00 PM – 11:00 PM

The Akoma Drummers are a group of percussionists, singers and dancers who present a fusion of African drumming and chants for a unique spiritual experience. The group formed in the spring of 2010 when Kristen Brooks brought their talent, bands and students together to play the djembe and djun djun drums from the wall of origins, from West African Mandingue culture. A portion of the proceeds from the event went to help HandReach.

A big thank you to Kristen Brooks, The Gail Line, and the New Deal Cafe for their generosity. The Gail Line is a rootsy DC-based swing duo with wide-ranging influences that include R&B, Rockabilly, Western Swing, Jump Blues, and Gypsy Jazz. They have forged a sound that embraces music history without sounding antiquated. The Akoma Drummers proudly study and present music of the African diaspora - the origin of all people and all music. Our music comes from traditional West African to funk and GO-GO. We play djembe, kenkeni, sangba, and dun dun drums, and spice up our compositions with percussion, dancing and songs. AKOMA describes the Abyssinia (ancient Ethiopia) region and represents the HEART. Adaba is Ghanaian for THE HEAVEN. Put them all together, and it means "The Heaven in the Heart." The HandReach Beat Brigade is looking Up From Poverty.

Burn-Injured Drummer Heals Youth in China with the Gift of Percussion

Washington Very Special Arts Society: 1100 13th Street, NW, Washington, DC
Thursday, June 17th, 2010, 7:00 – 9:00 PM

HandReach featured burn-injured Chinese drummer Dan Gao, who was burned over his body at age 2, kicked off his East Coast tour in Washington DC. Mr. Gao will teach drum classes in Baltimore, Washington DC, and New York. His performances include Chinese traditional drumming, as well as drumming performance at the event, and a documentary film about Dan Gao's inspirational story. Dan Gao's personal story and his unique performance style will leave you forever changed.

Executive Director, Dr. Brecken Chinn Swartz explains HandReach's commitment to psychosocial care and therapeutic drumming: "All of us, no matter who we are, have survived traumas; some more visible than others. Drumming together takes us out of our egos and into our hearts, providing a powerful nonverbal healing modality that helps burn survivors release emotional and physical pain associated with trauma. If you want to learn more about Dan, and to see how he is a life-changing role model, please visit www.dangao.com.
